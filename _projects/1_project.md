This project investigates **multilevel resistive switching dynamics** in hafnium oxide (HfO₂) thin films by controlling the crystalline phase and self-assembled nanochannels. The work aims to realize reliable multi-bit data storage in resistive RAM (RRAM) devices with precisely controlled resistance states.

Key contributions include:
- Demonstration of multilevel switching by modulating phase composition in HfO₂
- Role of self-assembled oxygen-vacancy-rich nanochannels in switching dynamics
- Characterization of set/reset voltages, endurance, and retention behavior
- Exploration of oxygen vacancy distribution using advanced spectroscopic tools (XPS, Raman)

This work resulted in a publication in *Small* (2024) and follow-up work on multi-bit RRAM in *Advanced Materials Technologies* (2025).

**Techniques used:** RF Sputtering, XRD, XPS, TEM, I-V characterization (Keysight B2902A, Keithley 4200SCS), Impedance Spectroscopy.
