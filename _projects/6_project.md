This project focuses on **engineering oxygen vacancy distributions** in sputtered HfO₂ thin films to enable reliable multi-bit resistive switching for RRAM applications. Controlled introduction and spatial distribution of oxygen vacancies provide the key mechanism for analog resistance tuning.

Key topics:
- Role of sputtering conditions (gas ratio, pressure, power) on vacancy formation
- Nyquist and Bode analysis of impedance spectra to identify charge transport mechanisms
- Equivalent circuit modeling of the resistive switching interface
- Endurance and retention characterization for multi-bit storage reliability

Published in *Advanced Materials Technologies* 10(22), e00289 (2025).

**Techniques used:** RF/DC Sputtering, XPS, XRD, EIS (Impedance Spectroscopy), Keithley 4200SCS.
