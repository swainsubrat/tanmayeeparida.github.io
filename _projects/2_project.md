This project explores **quantum transport phenomena** in hexagonal boron nitride (hBN) / hafnium oxide (HfO₂) bilayer memristive devices. The study reveals the formation of quantum point contacts during resistive switching, leading to quantized conductance — a key signature for atomic-scale filament formation.

Key contributions include:
- Observation of conductance quantization in integer multiples of G₀ = 2e²/h
- Identification of quantum-point-contact formation during filament evolution
- Correlation between switching dynamics and quantum transport signatures
- Potential for application in neuromorphic computing via analog conductance states

Published in *Nanotechnology* 37, 085001 (2026).

**Techniques used:** CVD-grown hBN, RF sputtering of HfO₂, SMU-based I-V characterization, Impedance Spectroscopy (EIS), AFM, TEM.
